Foundations

SNAP Basics

Purpose
Sign in, learn the layout, and understand how clients move through SNAP.
Who / When
Every bookkeeper · first login, then daily.

Logging in

  1. Go to snap.ironbooks.com and enter your @ironbooks.com email.
  2. SNAP is passwordless — you'll get a magic link by email. Click it to sign in.
  3. If a link expires, just request a new one from the login screen.

Navigating — the four spines

The sidebar is deliberately short. Collapse it to icons anytime with the toggle at the top.

NavWhat's there
HomeYour day. A greeting, a one-line summary, this week's wins, then three tabs: Today (daily review), Inbox (client messages), Tasks.
ClientsThe whole fleet in one filterable table — stage, status, assignee, attention badges. Filter to My clients.
PipelinesThe Onboarding / Cleanup / Production boards on one screen with a toggle.
Oversight seniorApprovals + Advisor + Fleet Health (SOP 16).
Admin adminThe control room + every fleet tool (SOP 17).

The client lifecycle — three stages

Every client's stage is derived automatically from the work done — you don't set it by hand.

StageWhat it means
OnboardingNew client — connect QuickBooks, capture the foundation, gather documents.
CleanupBring the books to correct — the 8-step sequence (SOP 4), then submit for review.
ProductionLive — daily reconciliation and monthly close.

Within Cleanup and Production, a client can also be Ready for review / Waiting on client (needs a human) or Failed review (bounced back to rework — SOP 12).

Roles

RoleCan do
ViewerRead-only.
BookkeeperDay-to-day work on any client (cleanup, reclass, BS, close).
Lead senior+ Oversight hub: review/approve cleanups & closes, reassign clients.
Admin seniorFull access + the Admin control room, user management, audit log, fleet tools.
💡
Daily rhythm: start on Home → Today — clear anything sent back to you and the daily review queue — then pick up cleanup work from Pipelines or Clients.

Onboarding

Onboarding a New Client

Purpose
Take a newly-won sale to "ready for cleanup" — mostly client self-serve.
Who / When
Assigned bookkeeper · when a new client lands.

How it works now — the client onboards themselves

New clients get a guided portal onboarding wizard. They watch a short video, complete the foundation intake, and mark their documents as sent. Their progress shows on the client's card so you can see where they are.

  1. A won sale creates the client automatically. Find them under Pipelines → Onboarding or in the Clients table (stage = Onboarding).
  2. Assign yourself (or a bookkeeper) as the owner from the card or the Clients table.
  3. Send the portal invite (Admin → Invite client, or the client card) so they can log in and run the wizard. Until they have a login they can't complete onboarding or receive statements.

Your setup pieces

  1. Connect QuickBooks (SOP 3) — nothing else can start without it.
  2. Review the foundation intake — the client's wizard answers auto-populate the profile (contact, legal name, corporate type, fiscal year-end, jurisdiction). Confirm them on the Profile tab; set jurisdiction correctly — it drives GST/HST and the CAD-vs-US payment link.
  3. Confirm the accounts & loans attestation ("these are all my accounts") came through — it's the basis for the cleanup scope.
💡
Stranded lead? If a client stalls in onboarding but you're ready to work, use Force to cleanup on their card to move them into the Cleanup stage manually.
⚠️
Set jurisdiction before cleanup — it controls sales-tax handling and which tax export applies. A client with no portal login can't receive messages, statements, or "your P&L is ready" alerts.

Onboarding

Connecting QuickBooks

Purpose
Authorize SNAP to read & write the client's QBO — the prerequisite for all cleanup and reporting.
Who / When
Assigned bookkeeper · during onboarding; reconnect if it ever expires.

Connect

  1. On the client's profile, the QBO banner shows connection health. If amber ("not connected"), click Connect QuickBooks.
  2. Sign in to Intuit, choose the right company file, and click Connect. You'll land back in SNAP — refresh once if the banner doesn't turn green.

Reconnect & diagnose

  1. A red banner ("expired") means the token aged out (~100 days) or the client revoked SNAP — click Reconnect and redo the flow. A QBO DISCONNECTED badge appears on the Clients table until it's fixed.
  2. Empty P&L? Check the banner first: green=connected, red=reconnect, amber=connect. It's the single source of truth — don't mistake an expired token for "no data."

The Cleanup Sequence

Purpose
One ordered checklist that takes a client from connected to clean books — no guessing what's next.
Who / When
Assigned bookkeeper · the whole Cleanup stage.

Open the client → Cleanup tab. Instead of hunting for tools, you get one ordered sequence. Each step deep-links the tool it needs, shows its status, and can be marked done or skipped. Work top to bottom; the checklist remembers where you left off.

#StepWhat you do
1Foundation & documentsStatements/docs on file, open the Balance Sheet — know what you're working with.
2Chart of accountsConform the chart to the master COA (SOP 5).
3Categorize transactionsAI reclassify, you review & approve (SOP 6).
4Revenue integrityCatch double-counted revenue (SOP 11).
5Expenses & duplicatesDuplicate-expense + payroll double-count scans (SOP 11).
6Balance sheetA/R, A/P, undeposited funds, loans, draws, bank rec (SOP 9).
7Bank rulesSave rules so it stays clean next month (SOP 7).
8Verify & first statementsRun verification, clear red flags, sign off (SOP 12).
💡
Steps 2–7 map to the SOPs that follow. Do them in order — categorizing against a messy chart just moves the mess around.
5
Cleanup · Step 2

Chart of Accounts Cleanup

Purpose
Standardize the client's chart to the master COA before categorizing anything.
Who / When
Assigned bookkeeper · first cleanup step after QBO connect.

Two ways in

ToolUse it for
COA Cleanup (New Cleanup job)The full reviewed per-client pass — retype, create, re-nest, merge duplicates, retire dead accounts. You review the plan, then it executes to QBO.
COA Audit /coa-auditThe fleet view: every client's conformance % vs the master, cleanup stage, and stranded $. One-click Fix all applies the standard chart. Scans are saved — "Scan new & stale" only re-checks what changed.
Master COAThe standard chart every client aligns toward.

What a merge does

  1. Merging a duplicate moves the real transactions onto the correct account (so the drill-down keeps its detail), sweeps anything the API can't line-move via itemized journal entries, re-points any items, and retires the emptied account.
  2. Done when conformance is high and stranded $ = 0 (no money left on retired accounts).
💡
Payroll is special. QuickBooks locks paycheques away from the API — SNAP (and even QBO's own Reclassify tool) can't move them. For payroll-sourced balances an adjusting JE is the only move; SNAP itemizes those JEs so the detail is preserved.
6
Cleanup · Step 3

Categorizing Transactions (Reclassify)

Purpose
Categorize the client's transactions against the cleaned COA, with AI assistance.
Who / When
Assigned bookkeeper · after the COA is in good shape.

Run → review → execute

  1. Start a reclassification from the client's Cleanup tab; pick the scope.
  2. On the Review screen, accept good AI proposals and correct the rest. Nothing posts yet.
  3. For big jobs, categorization runs in chunks — use the Continue gate to resume; progress is saved.
  4. Low-confidence items are held for review (they won't auto-approve). When you genuinely can't tell, use Ask Client rather than guessing.
  5. Execute to write the approved categorizations to QBO. Failed items surface for retry.
⚠️
Execute writes to live books — finish the review before running it. A few things can't be moved via the API (item-based lines, bank-matched, un-Added "For Review" items) — SNAP tells you which to handle in QuickBooks. A clean book has no "Uncategorized" bucket left.
7
Cleanup · Step 7

Bank Rules

Purpose
Turn categorization decisions into reusable QBO bank-feed rules so future months auto-categorize.
Who / When
Assigned bookkeeper · after a reclass job, or as a standalone pass.

Steps

  1. Build rules from a reclass job or via Bank Rules Discovery, which groups history by vendor.
  2. Review each proposed vendor → category rule; keep recurring vendors, skip one-offs.
  3. Export to QuickBooks — the .xls imports into the client's bank feed. Delete the client's old rules first so they don't conflict. The Admin Bank rules — fleet view tracks who's had rules applied.
⚠️
Exported rules act on the live feed — an over-broad rule miscategorizes future transactions automatically.

Cleanup

Stripe Reconciliation

Purpose
Match Stripe deposits to invoices, splitting out fees and sales tax.
Who / When
Assigned bookkeeper · clients who take payment via Stripe.

Steps

  1. Connect Stripe: send the client a Stripe Connect link to authorize. No Stripe? Mark it not required.
  2. Start a Stripe reconciliation; pick the date range. SNAP pulls deposits, matches them to invoices, and calculates fees + sales tax.
  3. Review each match and the fee/tax split, then Execute to post to QBO.
💡
Stripe deposits are net of fees — SNAP splits gross invoice, fee expense, and net deposit so everything reconciles.
9
Cleanup · Step 6

Balance Sheet Cleanup

Purpose
Reconcile the balance sheet account-by-account, using the right tool for each problem.
Who / When
Assigned bookkeeper · the BS step of cleanup.

Home base + specialized tools

  1. Work from the BS Cleanup Wizard (guided reconciliation), account by account.
  2. Reach for a specialized tool when an account is messy: A/R Recovery (unapplied payments), Uncategorized Income Recovery, UF Audit / UF-AI (orphan undeposited funds), Hardcore Cleanup (CRM-migration duplicate invoices).
  3. Each tool ends by posting balanced JEs to QBO — confirm they're balanced and correctly dated. SNAP guards closed periods and duplicate posting, but you confirm correctness.
💡
Follow the money: overstated A/R usually means cash landed in Uncategorized Income or Undeposited Funds. The recovery tools overlap — use them together.

Cleanup · Steps 4–5

Revenue & Expense Integrity

Purpose
Catch — and fix — money counted twice: duplicated revenue, duplicated expenses, payroll double-counts.
Who / When
Assigned bookkeeper · cleanup steps 4 & 5, and it's re-checked at month-end.

What SNAP flags

CheckThe problem
Revenue integrityA deposit booked as income on top of the invoice — the invoice+deposit double-count.
CRM invoice revenueCRM-pushed invoices double-counting deposit revenue — set the client to deposits-only, or void the phantom invoices.
Duplicate transactionsThe same expense posted twice — void the extra copy.
Payroll double-countThe gross paycheque and the net-pay bank withdrawal both expensed to labor.

Fixing it in QuickBooks

  1. From the client's Revenue check, review the flagged pairs. Always run the Preview / dry-run first — it shows exactly what will change without touching QBO.
  2. Choose per finding: void the duplicate (invoice + phantom payment) or keep the invoice and apply the matched deposit to it. Then apply.
  3. Anything with real cash on a payment is held for a human — it won't auto-void.
💡
These same checks power the red-flag gate at send time (SOP 14) — clearing them now means the month-end close won't get blocked later.

Cleanup · Step 8

Submit for Review & Failed Review

Purpose
Wrap a cleanup, get manager sign-off, and hand the client their first statements.
Who / When
Bookkeeper submits; Lead/Admin approves.

Submit

  1. When the sequence is done, Verify (run the checks) and submit for review — the client moves to Ready for review and lands in the senior's Oversight → Approvals queue.
  2. If the balance sheet isn't done but you want to move forward, defer BS — the client shows a BS OWED badge and stays flagged until it's cleaned.

Approve — or reject

  1. A Lead/Admin reviews and either approves (statements go out, client graduates toward Production) or rejects with a note.
  2. A rejected client moves to Failed review and bounces back onto your Home → Today under "Sent back — needs rework," with the manager's reason attached. Fix it and resubmit.
💡
Check Home → Today first thing every morning — a sent-back file is your highest-priority item.

Production

Production & Daily Reconciliation

Purpose
Promote a clean client to daily recon and work the queue each morning.
Who / When
Assigned bookkeeper · once a client is signed off, then daily.

Promote to production

  1. Approving the cleanup sign-off graduates the client to Production and turns on the nightly daily-recon run: confident new transactions auto-categorize; the rest go to Today.
  2. Before relying on it, dry-run the client on the Admin → Daily recon page — it previews what the engine would categorize without touching QuickBooks. You can Pause / Unpause / Disable recon anytime (with a reason shown to the team).

Work the Today queue

  1. Open Home → Today each morning. The nightly run has already posted the confident items; you review what it flagged.
  2. For each flagged item: accept or correct the account, then Approve (leave "Teach rule" on so that vendor auto-categorizes next time). Use Reject if it shouldn't post, Ask Client if you need info, and bulk-approve the obvious batch.
  3. Handle Client answers — when a client answers a transaction question from their portal, Approve applies their pick to QuickBooks in one click.
⚠️
Only production clients need daily attention — but a QBO disconnect stops the feed, so clear the reconnect prompt fast when you see it.

Month-End Close

Purpose
Close each production client's books every month and send statements.
Who / When
Assigned bookkeeper closes; Lead/Admin approves the send.
🚦
One way to close a month: the client's close card on the Pipelines → Production board. Sending statements is client-facing and can't be unsent, so every close runs the same gates — checks, statement review, attestation. No bulk send, no shortcut.

Steps

  1. Pipelines → Production — pick the close period; each client is a card (Not Started → In Progress → Blocked → Waiting on Client → Awaiting Review → Failed Review).
  2. Open a card → Run checks (uncategorized, undeposited funds, bank tie-outs, A/R…) and fix anything flagged — each failed check links to the right tool.
  3. Review the statements (P&L / BS / Cash Flow). Use "See client's view" to check exactly what the client will get, and pin review notes for the manager or your future self (internal-only — the client never sees them).
  4. Submit for review. A senior does the final close & send: the red-flag gate runs (duplicate revenue/expenses/payroll, COGS & margin sanity) and blocks the send until every flag is fixed or approved with a reason. Then tick the attestation box and confirm — that publishes to the portal, emails the client, and closes the QuickBooks period.
💡
Never approve past a red flag you don't understand. Approve-with-reason only after you've verified it's legitimate — the gate is what lets us move fast without breaking accuracy.

Ongoing

Client Service

Purpose
Report, communicate, follow up on calls, and manage billing — the ongoing relationship.
Who / When
Assigned bookkeeper · ongoing.

Reporting, messaging, calls, billing

  1. Reporting: review the P&L and Balance Sheet on the client's tabs (date ranges, drill-downs, % of income). Publish statements to the portal when ready.
  2. Messaging: one Ask-Client composer handles every client question through a single delivery path. Inbound replies surface in Home → Inbox.
  3. Calls: each client's call recordings show the AI summary + action items; work the open to-dos.
  4. Billing: the Billing tab shows tier, subscription, and invoice history; handle tier changes and cancels there.
⚠️
A client with no portal login can't see published reports or notifications — make sure they're invited (SOP 2).

Admin — the Control Room

Purpose
See what needs a decision, watch the money, and reach every fleet tool.
Who / When
Admin · daily scan + as needed.

What's on the Admin page

  1. Search — jump to any client or employee in two keystrokes.
  2. Exceptions — everything needing an admin decision: QBO disconnects, stuck jobs, failed reviews, failed jobs, open escalations, and a stale daily-recon engine warning if the nightly run hasn't fired. Each row links to the fix.
  3. Money at a glance — active clients, expected monthly revenue (MRR), and failed payments this month.
  4. Upgrade radar — clients that outgrew their tier · Bookkeeper scoreboard — closes & cleanups per person.

User management

  1. Header buttons: Employees and Clients (one click straight to the clients tab). Manage roles and impersonate / "view as client" from Users.
  2. Audit log — the full system event trail.

Escalating a Client (and Getting Your Answer)

Purpose
One way to say "a senior needs to look at this client" — and one place the answer comes back.
Who / When
Anyone on the team · the moment you're blocked on a judgment call.

Raising one, and where it goes

  1. On any board card or Clients row, click the flag button, optionally add a line of context, and pick a reason — that sends it. If the client's already escalated for the same thing, your note is added to it (nothing lost).
  2. The client gets a red ESCALATED badge everywhere, and seniors work the queue on Oversight → Approvals: who raised it, why, how old. Resolve is one click + an optional note.
  3. Resolved escalations stay visible for 7 days (greyed, with the resolution note) so you get your answer.
💡
Escalate when work on a client is blocked on a decision or a senior's eyes. Day-to-day questions go through normal messaging.

Attention Badges — What They Mean

Purpose
Read a client's attention state at a glance. Badges are automatic — nobody maintains them.
Who / When
Everyone · on the Clients table and the Pipelines boards.

The badges (worst first)

  1. ESCALATED (red) — someone flagged this client for a senior (SOP 18). Darker red = open >3 days.
  2. QBO DISCONNECTED (red) — the connection is dead; nothing syncs until the client reconnects.
  3. FAILED REVIEW (red) — a manager sent the cleanup/close back to rework.
  4. BILLING HOLD / PAST DUE / UNBILLED — billing state (seniors see all; bookkeepers see HOLD). UNBILLED in production = flag to Mike.
  5. BS OWED (gold) — on P&L-only service; the balance-sheet cleanup is still owed.
  6. QUEUE · Nd OLD (gold) — the daily-review queue is falling behind SLA.
💡
Every board and the Clients table has a Flagged only filter — your triage view on a fat list. A badge is a state, not a task; if it needs a decision you can't make, that's what the flag button is for.
